Things I Would Change About My Country

Every country has beautiful traditions, landscapes, and people with warm hearts. My country is no exception. I feel proud of many things, but I also see daily problems that make life difficult for workers, families, students, and older adults. If I could change certain things, I would focus on the everyday issues that affect people the most. These changes would help us live with more dignity, stability, and peace.

1. Stronger Public Services

One of the first things I wish I could change is the quality of public services. Many families in my country experience interruptions of water or electricity, sometimes for long hours. Hospitals and clinics often lack essential medicines, equipment, or trained staff. These problems make simple daily activities stressful. According to several reports, the health system has struggled for years due to shortages and lack of investment (Human Rights Watch).

Reliable services would give people more confidence and allow them to focus on their work, studies, or personal goals instead of worrying about basic needs.

2. A Fair and Stable Economy

Another change I dream of is economic stability. Inflation has affected many families, making food, medicine, and rent more expensive every month. Many people work long hours yet still cannot save money or make long-term plans. News outlets have often described the economic situation as fragile and unstable, with millions of people struggling to cover basic living costs (El País International).

I would like to see a country where salaries allow people to live comfortably, where prices are reasonable, and where young people do not feel forced to leave the country to find opportunities.

3. Better Schools and Real Opportunities for Students

Education is a key part of any strong society. But many schools lack books, computers, or even safe buildings. Some classrooms are too hot, too crowded, or poorly maintained. Teachers work hard, but they often do not receive enough support.

I would change this by investing in modern schools, with updated materials, access to technology, and well-trained teachers. Students should have opportunities to learn languages, science, art, and sports. A good education system gives young people the power to build a better future.

4. Safe Streets and Peaceful Communities

Safety is another important issue. Many people feel afraid to walk alone at night or to let their children play outside. Even small crimes affect daily life: robberies, noise, vandalism, or fights. These problems create fear and insecurity.

I imagine neighborhoods with good lighting, clean public spaces, strong community programs, and respectful police work. When people feel safe, everything changes: children can play outside, businesses grow, and communities become stronger.

5. Reducing Corruption and Increasing Transparency

One of the biggest challenges in my country is corruption. It can be found in different areas of public life: institutions, services, or government offices. Many people feel that money meant for hospitals, schools, or infrastructure is sometimes misused or lost. This destroys trust and slows down progress (The Guardian – World News).

If I could change something, it would be to create transparent systems where decisions are clear and money is used responsibly. A country grows when leaders act with honesty and when people trust their institutions.

6. Affordable Living Conditions

The cost of living in many cities has increased a lot. Food, transportation, clothes, and even basic services can become too expensive. Some families live day-to-day, without the chance to save or invest in their future. This creates stress and uncertainty.

I wish that people could enjoy fair prices, reasonable rents, and access to affordable food and medicine. A country is stronger when its people can live with dignity and stability.

7. Cleaner Cities and Respect for the Environment

Finally, I would love to see cleaner streets, recycling programs, and care for rivers, parks, and beaches. Pollution and trash are problems in many areas. Public spaces should be places where families can relax, exercise, or enjoy nature.

I believe small actions can create big changes: throwing trash in the right place, using less plastic, planting trees, and protecting natural areas. A clean environment improves health and happiness.
